Internal Business Review - Q4 2025 (October to December 2025)

Revenue declined sharply in Q4 2025 compared to Q3 2025 across all regions. The primary
drivers identified by the leadership team were:

1. Loss of key account: Meridian Retail Group, our second-largest customer (roughly 18%
   of quarterly revenue), did not renew their contract in late September 2025 after being
   acquired by a competitor-aligned holding company.

2. Marketing budget cut: As a cost-control measure, the marketing spend was reduced by
   approximately 40% starting October 2025. Lead generation dropped within three weeks,
   directly reducing new sales of Widget A and Widget B.

3. Supply chain disruption: A port strike in October 2025 delayed Widget C inventory by
   five weeks, causing stockouts in the West and East regions during peak demand.

4. Seasonal softness: Q4 is historically our weakest quarter, but the decline in 2025
   (about -35% quarter-over-quarter) was far deeper than the typical seasonal dip of -10%.

Recovery actions taken in Q1 2026: marketing budget restored in January, two new
enterprise accounts signed (Apex Logistics, Northwind Foods), and a secondary supplier
onboarded for Widget C. Revenue recovered steadily from January 2026 onward.
